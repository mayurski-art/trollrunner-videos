# MANGO EMPIRE: The Troll Behind It All
### A 3-Part AI Animated Crime-Thriller Series (YouTube Shorts / TikTok)

Your original 12-scene script had a genuinely strong hook (anthropomorphic
fruit cartel + a hidden Trollface puppet master) but it was structured as one
long linear story. Long-form linear scripts underperform on Shorts/TikTok
because the algorithm rewards **rewatch rate** and **series follows**, not
one-and-done viewing. The fix below keeps every scene and line you wrote (with
sharper dialogue) but restructures it into **3 cliffhanger-ending parts**, adds
a locked character bible for visual consistency across generations, and adds
the production/posting details that actually drive revenue.

---

## 1. Why this restructure works

- **Cliffhangers > one long video.** Splitting into Part 1/2/3 with a "wait,
  WHAT?" cut at the end of each part forces follows and comments ("post part
  2!!"), which is the single biggest lever for the TikTok/Shorts algorithm.
- **First 1.5 seconds decide everything.** Each part now opens on the single
  most shocking frame instead of a slow establishing shot, with on-screen text
  doing the hook's work before the VO even starts.
- **Consistency = believability.** AI image/video tools (Midjourney, SDXL,
  Kling, Runway Gen-3, Pika, Sora) drift between generations. The character
  bible below is a fixed block of descriptive text you paste into *every*
  prompt for a character so the audience can track who's who across 12 shots.
- **Quotable lines get clipped.** Dialogue has been tightened so at least one
  line per part is screenshot/clip-bait on its own.

## 2. Character Bible (lock this — paste into every relevant prompt)

> **MANGO BOSS** — anthropomorphic mango with a smooth glossy orange-yellow
> head, faint dimpled mango skin texture, slicked-back hair, smug confident
> smirk, gold chain, gold watch, unbuttoned white silk shirt, black tailored
> trousers. Cartel-kingpin posture: relaxed but always in control.

> **CHERRY** — anthropomorphic cherry with glossy deep-red head, short green
> stem, playful seductive expression, red satin dress, confident hip-forward
> walk. Reads as dangerous-charming, never just decorative.

> **BROCCOLI AGENT** — anthropomorphic broccoli floret head with visible
> textured crown, hardened federal-agent face, tactical vest worn over a dark
> suit, earpiece mic, serious commanding presence.

> **STRAWBERRY DETECTIVE** — anthropomorphic strawberry head with visible
> seed texture, worn fedora, beige trench coat, red gloves, noir
> private-investigator energy, revolver, perpetually exhausted/obsessed eyes.

> **ZUCCHINI ENFORCER** — anthropomorphic elongated dark-green zucchini body
> with natural vegetable texture and subtle curvature, fully humanoid,
> muscular build, fitted black suit, cold expressionless enforcer face.

> **GRAPE INFORMANT** — clustered purple-grape head, fully anthropomorphic
> (never clipart-flat), white dress shirt, loosened tie, torn suit jacket,
> dress shoes, permanently panicked expression.

> **TROLLFACE** — classic black-and-white meme face rendered as a *solid
> mask-like surface* (no exposed human skin, no scalp, no extra nose), black
> sunglasses, black tailored suit, cigarette smoke curling past the grin,
> always partially obscured by shadow, fog, or screen glitch. He is never
> fully shown in good lighting until the final reveal in Part 3 — that
> restraint is what sells the twist.

**Style line to append to every image prompt:**
`cohesive high-budget animated crime-thriller style, dramatic cinematic
lighting, realistic reflections and shadows, shallow depth of field,
meme-noir atmosphere, consistent character design, no text artifacts unless
specified`

## 3. Production notes (read before generating)

- **Tools:** Midjourney v6/Niji or SDXL for key art → Runway Gen-3, Kling 1.6,
  or Pika for image-to-video motion → ElevenLabs (or Eleven's voice-design)
  for the VO cast → CapCut for captions, pacing, and trending-audio layering.
- **Clip length:** 2.5–4s per shot. A 4-scene part should land at 35–55s —
  the sweet spot for both Shorts and TikTok completion rate.
- **Captions:** Burn in bold, high-contrast captions (CapCut "Karaoke" style)
  for every VO line. Most viewers watch muted first.
- **Audio:** Open each part on a trending TikTok/Reels sound for the first
  0.5–1s *before* the VO line lands — this is currently the single biggest
  unused lever in your script. Use a tense/cinematic trending sound, not
  comedy audio, to match tone.
- **Thumbnails/cover frames:** Use the Trollface-glimpse frame from each part
  (balcony reflection, yacht hull emblem, limousine reveal) as the cover —
  curiosity gap sells the click.
- **Monetization reality check:** YouTube Shorts needs 1,000 subscribers +
  10M public Shorts views in 90 days (or watch-hour path) for the Partner
  Program. TikTok Creator Rewards needs videos **over 1 minute**, 10K
  followers, and 100K views in the last 30 days. That's why each part below
  is built to land at ~55–75s when you add a 5–8s cold-open hook + outro CTA —
  long enough to qualify, short enough to hold retention.
- **Posting cadence:** Post Part 1, pin a comment teasing Part 2, post Part 2
  24–48h later referencing the comments from Part 1, then Part 3 as the
  "finale" — series payoff videos consistently outperform standalone posts.

## 5. The Script

### PART 1 — "The Empire" (Scenes 1–4)

**Cold open (0–2s, no VO):** Hard cut to the Scene 1 balcony shot, already
zoomed in tight on the faint Trollface reflection in the glass.
**On-screen text:** `He thinks he runs this island. He's wrong.`

**Scene 1**
*Image:* A slick mango boss in a white silk shirt stands on a high balcony
overlooking a massive tropical cartel island at sunset. Below: stacked cargo
crates, armed guards, glowing neon docks, ocean waves. Luxurious but tense.
In the reflective glass behind him, a faint distorted Trollface silhouette is
embedded in the reflection — present but not fully visible.
*Video:* Slow aerial push toward the balcony, ocean wind moving his silk
shirt. Camera nears the glass; the reflection stays just out of focus.
*VO (MANGO BOSS, calm, intimidating):* "I built this empire brick by brick.
Nobody — nobody — is taking it from me."

**Scene 2**
*Image:* A seductive cherry in a red dress walks past armed guards on a neon
dock, handing a glowing vial to Mango Boss. In the background, a black luxury
yacht arrives, a faint painted Trollface grin emblem on its hull.
*Video:* Smooth tracking shot as the yacht glides into the dock lights, the
hull emblem catching the neon for one frame.
*VO (CHERRY, playful, seductive):* "Your shipment just docked, handsome. Try
not to bruise it."

**Scene 3**
*Image:* A broccoli agent slams surveillance photos on a table in a dark ops
room. One photo: Mango Boss getting into a car. Another, blurred: a
muscular figure in a suit shaking hands with cartel buyers underground.
*Video:* Quick push-in on the blurred photo — just enough to register a
Trollface grin among the shadows before it's covered by another file.
*VO (BROCCOLI AGENT, serious):* "We've got eyes on the compound. Tonight, we
end this."

**Scene 4**
*Image:* Strawberry detective loads a revolver at her desk. A folder labeled
**"TROLLFACE — UNKNOWN ORIGIN"** stamped in red sits open in front of her.
*Video:* Slow orbit around the desk, ending on the stamped file.
**On-screen text (last frame):** `Part 2 →`
*VO (STRAWBERRY DETECTIVE, intense):* "Two years chasing this mango. He
doesn't walk free — not this time."

### PART 2 — "The Warning" (Scenes 5–8)

**Cold open (0–2s):** Hard cut straight into the dock-violence beat, already
mid-action, no slow build.
**On-screen text:** `He warned everyone what happens to rats.`

**Scene 5**
*Image:* A dark, rain-slicked dock at night. A muscular anthropomorphic
zucchini enforcer in a fitted black suit drags a terrified anthropomorphic
grape informant toward the edge — white shirt, loosened tie, torn jacket,
genuinely resisting, not posed. Neon harbor lights reflect in the wet stone.
In the background, a black limousine arrives through fog. Trollface stands
beside it: classic b&w meme face, sunglasses, black suit, cigarette smoke,
giving the middle finger with a grin, half-swallowed by shadow.
*Video:* The zucchini hauls the grape back from the railing as rain hits the
water. Limousine headlights slice through the fog. Trollface flips them off,
grinning, then steps back into darkness.
*VO (ZUCCHINI ENFORCER, cold):* "You went to the cops about the troll? You
don't get to tell that story."

**Scene 6**
*Image:* A broccoli-led squad moves through dense jungle at night, tactical
gear, fog rays cutting through the canopy. The lead agent holds a glowing
radio device; static across it warps for a frame into a grinning Trollface
shape.
*Video:* Tight, urgent movement through foliage; radio static briefly forms
the Trollface laugh distortion, then clears.
*VO (BROCCOLI AGENT, urgent whisper):* "Stay low. If they spot us, we're
mulch."

**Scene 7**
*Image:* Compound control room in full lockdown — red emergency light across
glossy monitors, guards scrambling, Mango Boss cutting through the center
with a leather briefcase. The main screen reads **"TROLL PROTOCOL ACTIVE"**
as static corrupts the feed.
*Video:* Alarms strobe red as the override triggers; the room reacts a beat
*before* Mango Boss even reaches for the screen — he didn't trigger this.
*VO (MANGO BOSS, furious):* "Lock it down! Nobody leaves till I say so!"

**Scene 8**
*Image:* A gold-lit luxury apartment. Mango Boss and a visibly pregnant
Cherry relax in wealth. A contract on the table is signed with nothing but a
small Trollface mark instead of a name.
*Video:* Slow pan across the room, ending on the signature mark held a beat
too long.
**On-screen text (last frame):** `Part 3 →`
*VO (MANGO BOSS, smug):* "Our kid's gonna be born into pure profit."

---

### PART 3 — "Too Ripe to Fall" (Scenes 9–12)

**Cold open (0–2s):** Open already on the empty ocean from Scene 11 — show
the loss before explaining it, then rewind into the chase.
**On-screen text:** `She almost had him. Almost.`

**Scene 9**
*Image:* Strawberry detective stands in the rain outside the docks. Behind
her, a billboard flickers — for one frame it shows Trollface smiling in
clean corporate branding, then glitches back to a normal ad.
*Video:* Slow tilt up from her face to the glitching billboard.
*VO (STRAWBERRY DETECTIVE, broken):* "I really thought this would end it."

**Scene 10**
*Image:* A speedboat escape at night — Mango Boss and the Zucchini enforcer
fleeing. The dashboard nav system reads **"TROLL ROUTE ENABLED"** in a cold
glitching font.
*Video:* The boat carves through dark water as the nav screen locks onto a
route neither of them chose.
*VO (ZUCCHINI ENFORCER, shouting):* "Go, go, go — she's got the engine hot!"

**Scene 11**
*Image:* Strawberry detective arrives at the now-empty dock. A crate drifts
in the water, a painted Trollface grin on its surface, the only thing left
behind.
*Video:* Slow push toward the drifting crate.
*VO (STRAWBERRY DETECTIVE, defeated):* "No. They're gone."

**Scene 12 — The Reveal**
*Image:* Mango Boss laughs alone on the boat at night. In the ocean fog
behind him, shadows resolve into a single massive Trollface silhouette,
dwarfing the boat — for the first time, fully formed, fully lit, watching
everything.
*Video:* Slow pull-back as the enormous Trollface shadow rises out of the
fog. Mango Boss never turns around — he doesn't know it's there. The audience
does.
*VO (MANGO BOSS, laughing):* "You can't peel me. I'm too ripe for this
world."
**On-screen text (final frame, hold 2s):** `He was never the boss.`
**End card:** `Follow for the full story` + series logo.
